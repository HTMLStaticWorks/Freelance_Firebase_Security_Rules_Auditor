Security Philosophy

Security shouldn't be an afterthought. It must be woven into the fabric of your data architecture.

Default Deny

Every collection, document, and field is locked down by default. Access is explicitly granted only when necessary, minimizing the attack surface.

Granular Validation

Rules aren't just for access. They are the ultimate schema enforcers, ensuring data integrity by validating types, lengths, and constraints.

Performance Aware

Complex rules can slow down database reads. My implementations balance absolute security with optimized execution paths to maintain high performance.
